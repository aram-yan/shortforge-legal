Privacy Policy — ShortForge

Last updated: 2026-06-09

ShortForge ("the App") is a personal tool operated by an individual creator ("the Operator") to prepare and publish short-form videos to the Operator's own social media accounts (YouTube, TikTok, Instagram). This policy explains what data the App accesses and how it is used.

Who this applies to

The App is used solely by the Operator to manage the Operator's own content and accounts. It is not offered as a service to other users and does not collect data from the public.

Information the App accesses

With the Operator's explicit authorization (via each platform's official OAuth login), the App accesses only what is needed to publish video:

  • Basic account/channel information (e.g., account name and ID) to confirm the correct target account.
  • Content-publishing permission (e.g., TikTok video.publish, YouTube youtube.upload, Instagram content publishing) to upload videos the Operator has created or selected.

The App does not read your private messages, browsing activity, contacts, or any data beyond what the granted permissions cover.

How information is used

Accessed information is used for one purpose only: to publish the Operator's videos and associated metadata (titles, descriptions, hashtags) to the Operator's own accounts. It is not used for advertising, profiling, or any unrelated purpose.

Storage and security

  • Authorization tokens are stored locally on the Operator's own computer and are used only to call the platforms' official APIs.
  • Tokens are never sold, shared, or transmitted to any third party by the App.

Third-party processing

To produce the videos, content may be processed by third-party services the Operator chooses (for example: Google Cloud Translation for subtitles, ElevenLabs for voice, and Anthropic Claude for generating titles/descriptions). Such processing is governed by those providers' own privacy policies. No platform account credentials are shared with these services.

Data retention and deletion

  • The Operator can revoke the App's access at any time in the platform's settings (e.g., TikTok → Settings → Security → Manage app permissions; Google Account → Security → Third-party access).
  • Locally stored tokens can be deleted by the Operator at any time.

Children's privacy

The App is not directed to children and does not knowingly process data of anyone under 13.

Changes

This policy may be updated; the "Last updated" date will change accordingly.
